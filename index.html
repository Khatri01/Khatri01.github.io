About

I am a Ph.D. student in Computer Science and a Graduate Research Assistant at the Water & Environmental Systems Analysis (WESA) Lab, Texas A&M University–Corpus Christi. My research lies at the intersection of hydrology, hydraulics, and artificial intelligence. I develop machine learning and graph neural network models for rainfall-derived inflow and infiltration (RDII) quantification in wastewater networks, and surrogate modeling of sewer systems.

Previously, I earned my MSc in Water Resources and Environmental Management from Leibniz University Hannover, Germany, where I worked on hydrological extremes, hydro-numerical simulations, and sediment transport studies. My background also includes a B.Tech. in Civil Engineering, alongside professional experience in hydraulic and hydropower project design in Nepal.
